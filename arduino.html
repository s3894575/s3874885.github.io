What is it used for?

Microcomputers are exactly that, a small computer that has a microprocessor as a CPU, inputs, and outputs, and memory. These are usually relatively inexpensive, but that is more dependent on the model of the computers. This means anything that has an onboard system built-in is a microcomputer, but the small computing devices that are going to be discussed here are closer to being small PCs rather than something like an electronic lock, that is fixed, but rather these small computing devices allow for users to program and express their creativity.

Raspberry PI

The Raspberry Pi is the result of the work of the Raspberry Pi Foundation, an educational charity that is dedicated to making computing accessible to people, and the desire to promote the study of computer science in schools.

The Raspberry Pi is a small computer that runs Raspbian, a special version of Linux, and due to its size, it’s great for cool projects such as program robots, control systems in your house, or my favorite thing I’ve seen done with a Pi, have a fully contained PC (which the Pi is) but have it contained within a mouse.

The newest Raspberry Pi is the Raspberry Pi 4 Model B, but with the rapid innovation in pc technology, this will likely be not the case with a newer version coming out soon or so. The whole reason the raspberry pi can exist is due to advancements in low-end computing, and the desire to have a practically disposable computer that would allow for users to create what they wanted, with there being options for different expansions on the base model that Pi owners could use in their projects.

Arduino

Arduinos, unlike the Raspberry Pi, is not a self-contained pc with an OS, Arduinos are more like a microcontroller where you program it with a set of instructions than the Arduino will perform these instructions, being self-contained after initial programming. The programming language used on is a modified version of C++, and with this, Arduino users are free to program the unit to do what they want, like for example, using the Arduino to wirelessly control parts of your house, such as lights, remotely operate motors on a robot arm for example or even use it as an RFID scanner through the use of different arduinos with certain IO or through the use of shields (expansion cards). From the way the Arduino is described, these sorts of microcontrollers are everywhere, with devices programmed with specific instructions, then after that, the device performs its function. The reason the Arduino is special, due to its stand-alone system that users can buy and use for personal projects.

Makey Makey

The Makey Makey is a kit based around a microcontroller that allows for users to interact with everyday objects and allows the user to make them into keyboard and mouse inputs as long as it allows for an electric current to pass. The Makey Makey is limited to this feature, with the microcontroller only doing this, but there is a lot of interesting things that could be done with this feature alone.
