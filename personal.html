Personal Profile

Meyer Briggs Test

My personality results from the Meyer Briggs test suggest I am someone who is more likely to think before I act. It says I am more introverted than I am extraverted, which I can confirm is true about myself. However, it also suggests that I choose to be more intuitive than observative. This means that when working on projects in teams I might be more likely to try out my own ideas and test them before pitching them to the team. Moreover, it says I have a slightly higher tendency to be prospecting rather than judging, suggesting that I would rather try new ideas out than judge existing ones. Taking this into account, I should try to be more immediate in decision making and take more risks to learn from in design.

Learning Style

This also supplements well with my learning style test results. The test concluded that my learning style favoured Tactile the most which matches my experience the most. As much as someone can describe riding a bike or driving a car, you actually have to do it to learn. In an IT context, learning new systems does require some theory, but also using the system is necessary and essential to learning. Taking this into account, I should consider forming a team of different experiences to build a synergy around sharing skills allowing all to learn from each other.

Big Five Personality
